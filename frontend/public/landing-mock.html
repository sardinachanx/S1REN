S1REN

Simplifying emergency response on social media

Life-Threatening Problems

911 is down

During Hurricane Harvey and other recent natural disasters, people flocked to Twitter to ask for aid, express sympathy, post safety tips, and more.

Too much noise

If first responders want to use Twitter as a rescue tool, they would need to sort through lots of noise.

An Innovative Solution

Easily triage rescue requests

S1REN automatically loads tweets containing certain keywords and locations, then detects potential rescue requests using machine learning.

Address requests intuitively

S1REN presents potential requests for aid within an intuitive interface, letting humans dispatch them or flag them as false alarms.

Efficient resource allocation

Geographic data is extracted from tweets and mapped, allowing users to address the needs of high-priority, high-density areas

Early incident detection

The real-time and flexible nature of S1REN means that it can be used for any emergency and even to detect budding events, saving money and time
